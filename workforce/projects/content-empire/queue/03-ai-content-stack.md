### Script:

**[0:00-0:45] HOOK**
"In 2023, creating a single YouTube video took me 20+ hours. Research, scripting, filming, editing, thumbnails, SEO optimization—the whole process was exhausting.

Today? I can produce the same quality content in 4 hours. That's not because I got faster. It's because I built an AI-powered content creation stack that handles 80% of the repetitive work.

In this video, I'm breaking down every single tool I use, exactly how I use it, and how you can build your own AI content workflow."

**[0:45-2:00] THE OLD WAY VS THE NEW WAY**
"Let me show you the contrast. Here's my old workflow:

Research: 3-4 hours digging through articles, reports, trends
Scripting: 4-5 hours writing, rewriting, optimizing
Filming: 2-3 hours (multiple takes, flubs, retakes)
Editing: 8-10 hours cutting, adding B-roll, graphics, color grading
Thumbnails: 2-3 hours design and iteration
SEO: 1-2 hours titles, descriptions, tags

Total: 20-25 hours per video.

Now here's my current workflow with AI:

Research: 45 minutes (AI research assistants)
Scripting: 1 hour (AI first draft + my refinement)
Filming: 1 hour (teleprompter + AI editing handles flubs)
Editing: 1.5 hours (AI transcription, auto-editing, templates)
Thumbnails: 15 minutes (AI image generation + templates)
SEO: 10 minutes (AI optimization tools)

Total: 4-5 hours per video. Same quality. 5x faster."

**[2:00-4:30] THE RESEARCH STACK**
"Let's start with research. This used to be my biggest time sink.

TOOL 1: Perplexity AI
Instead of opening 20 browser tabs, I ask Perplexity complex research questions with citations. 'What are the latest trends in AI job markets with data from 2025?' I get synthesized answers with sources I can verify.

Time saved: 2-3 hours per video.

TOOL 2: Glimpse/Google Trends
For trending topics, I use Glimpse to identify rising search queries before they peak. This helps me create content that rides the wave instead of missing it.

TOOL 3: VidIQ/TubeBuddy
These tools analyze competitor videos, suggest titles with high CTR potential, and identify content gaps. I don't guess what works—I use data.

The key insight: AI doesn't replace my research judgment. It accelerates my information gathering so I can spend more time on analysis and perspective."

**[4:30-7:00] THE SCRIPTING & WRITING STACK**
"Next: scripting. This is where most creators get stuck, staring at a blank page.

TOOL 1: Claude/ChatGPT
I don't use AI to write my scripts. I use it to BREAK my scripts. Here's my process:

I write my main points and structure. Then I ask Claude: 'Act as a YouTube script editor. Here are my key points. Help me identify weak arguments, suggest stronger hooks, and find logical gaps.'

It becomes a sparring partner, not a ghostwriter.

TOOL 2: Descript (Storyboard)
For shorter content, I sometimes use Descript's AI to transcribe my rough thoughts, then edit the text directly. It's like writing by speaking.

TOOL 3: Grammarly Premium
Yes, Grammarly uses AI. The clarity suggestions and tone adjustments save me 30 minutes of editing per script.

The philosophy: AI amplifies my voice. It doesn't replace it."

**[7:00-9:30] THE PRODUCTION & EDITING STACK**
"Now the heavy lifting—editing. This was always 40% of my time.

TOOL 1: Descript
Game. Changer. I edit video by editing text. Remove filler words with one click. Cut sections by deleting sentences. Find 'good takes' by searching transcripts.

But here's what most people miss: Descript's AI eye contact correction. I can read from a script off-camera and it makes me look like I'm looking directly at the lens. No more teleprompter anxiety.

TOOL 2: CapCut (with AI features)
For short-form content, CapCut's auto-captions, background removal, and AI effects are incredible. I can create TikTok-ready content in 10 minutes.

TOOL 3: Adobe Premiere Pro + AI plugins
For complex edits, I still use Premiere. But with AI plugins like AutoPod for multicam editing and automated B-roll suggestions from Storyblocks, my workflow is 3x faster.

TOOL 4: ElevenLabs
For voiceovers when I'm sick or traveling, I can clone my voice and generate narration. I disclose when I use this, but it saves me during tight deadlines."

**[9:30-11:30] THE OPTIMIZATION STACK**
"Finally, the work that most creators skip but shouldn't.

TOOL 1: Midjourney + Photoshop AI
My thumbnail workflow: Generate 10+ concepts in Midjourney. Select the best. Upscale. Touch up in Photoshop with Generative Fill. Add text in Canva.

Result: Professional thumbnails in 15 minutes instead of 3 hours.

TOOL 2: VidIQ/TubeBuddy (again)
AI-powered title suggestions, description optimization, tag recommendations. These tools analyze millions of videos to predict what will perform.

TOOL 3: Repurpose.io
One long-form video becomes 10+ short pieces automatically. It clips highlights, resizes for different platforms, and even suggests captions.

This is how I maintain presence on YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter without working 80-hour weeks."

**[11:30-13:30] THE INTEGRATION: HOW IT ALL CONNECTS**
"Here's my actual weekly workflow:

MONDAY: Research day. Perplexity + trends analysis. I identify 3-4 video topics for the week.

TUESDAY: Scripting. Outline with my own thinking, use Claude as a sparring partner, refine until it sounds like me.

WEDNESDAY: Batch filming. 3-4 videos in one session using teleprompter and Descript eye contact.

THURSDAY: Edit day. Descript for rough cuts, Premiere for polish, CapCut for shorts.

FRIDAY: Optimization. Thumbnails, SEO, scheduling, repurposing.

The result: 3-4 high-quality videos per week in 25-30 hours. Before AI, that same output would have been 60-80 hours.

But here's the important part: I'm not creating MORE content. I'm creating the SAME content with more energy left for community engagement, strategy, and—shockingly—having a life."

**[13:30-15:00] WHO SHOULD USE THIS STACK**
"Should YOU adopt all these tools?

If you're just starting: Focus on ONE tool. Master Descript or CapCut first. Don't overwhelm yourself.

If you're growing: Add research and optimization tools. The time savings compound as you scale.

If you're full-time: Build the complete stack. Your competitive advantage is output quality AND consistency.

The tools I mentioned:
- Perplexity (research)
- Claude/ChatGPT (script sparring)
- Descript (editing + eye contact)
- CapCut (short-form)
- Midjourney (thumbnails)
- VidIQ/TubeBuddy (SEO)
- Repurpose.io (distribution)

Links in the description. Some are affiliate links—full transparency.

What's your biggest time sink in content creation? Drop it in the comments and I'll suggest an AI solution."
